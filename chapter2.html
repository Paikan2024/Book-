Return Without Applause

Where the road ends, the real war begins.

He returned not as a son welcomed, but as a piece moved too far. The city gates opened—then the silence did.

In the halls of stone and perfume, victory was recited like poetry, and every word was measured for its weight against his name.

The court smiled with the caution of people who have survived kings. They praised the army, thanked the banners, honored the “fortunate weather.” They did not say: the prince held the line.

Because a man who can hold a line can also draw a new one.
